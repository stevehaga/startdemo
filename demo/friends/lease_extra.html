What Would You Do?

PROBLEM:
You are considering paying down your auto lease in order to take advantage of the reduction of time and the interest return you will receive on the extra payment. Your lease is for $35,000 with a residual value of $15,000 due and payable in 48 months. You have determined that your interest rate is 16%. What would be your lease balance and the interest % return if you added $100 for 12 months?

SOLUTION:
With the FinancialCalc Lease Calculator with Extra Payment you can see that you would have a balance of $31,122 without the extra payment and $29,830 with. This is a difference of $1,292. The interest paid without the extra payment is $5,324 and $5,232 with. This is a interest savings of $92 on $1,200 of extra payments. The return on your $1,200 would be 7.67%. And this is a guaranteed return because the interest is reduced immediately each month.

You can also see by putting 39 months in the "Payments Made" cell that the lease will be paid off 9 months early. This way you will have paid a total of $33,421 in payments - $3,800 of which is the extra payment that has received a 7.67% return. And over the full term of the lease you will save $3,486 in total cash outlay for the extra payment of $3,900 in 39 months.

 

 

 

 

 

QuickFacts:

Power of the Extra Payment

Many times we have considered the best way to pay off our debts. Should we just borrow the money and pay off the debts with a lower interest rate loan? Should we refinance? Or in the case of a lease, should we apply additional payments to the lease? Which of these is the most frugal use of our cash flow?

In today's financial marketplace leases often carry very high interest charges. This is common especially with equipment or when companies have not established credit.

By making extra payments you have 3 major benefits:
1. You reduce the outstanding balance of the lease
2. Reduce the length of time left to pay

But the most significant is:
3. You receive immediate guaranteed return on your extra payment equal to approximately 1/2 of the interest rate you are paying on the lease. This is realized in the next months reduction of the interest due.
