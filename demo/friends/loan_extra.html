What would you do?

PROBLEM:
You are considering paying down a high interest personal loan in order to take advantage of the reduction of time and the interest return you will receive on the extra payment. Your loan is for $18,000, without a balloon payment due, and payable in 60 months. You know your present interest is 18%. What would be your loan balance and the interest % return on the extra payment in 12 months if you added $50 to the payment?

SOLUTION:
With the FinancialCalc Loan Calculator with Extra Payment you can see that after the first year you would have a balance of $15,560 without the extra payment and $14,908 with. This is a difference of $652. The interest paid without the extra payment is $3,045 and $2,993 with. This is a interest savings of $52 on $600 of extra payments. The return on your $600 would be 8.34%. And this is a guaranteed return because the interest is reduced immediately each month.

You can also see by putting 51 months in the "Payments Made" cell that the loan will be paid off 9 months early. This way you will have paid a total of $25,893 in payments - $2,550 of which is the extra payment that has received a 7.67% return. And over the full term of the loan you will save $1,532 in total cash outlay for the extra payment of $2,550 in 51 months.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

QuickFacts:

Power of the Extra Payment

Many times we have considered the best way to pay off our debts. Should we just borrow the money and pay off the debts with a lower interest rate loan? Should we refinance? Should we apply additional payments to the loan? Which of these is the most frugal use of our cash flow?

In today's financial market place loans often carry very high interest charges. This is normal especially when purchasing equipment or when good credit has not been established.

By making extra payments you have 3 major benefits:
1. You reduce the outstanding balance of the loan.
2. Reduce the length of time left to pay.

But the most significant is:
3. You receive immediate guaranteed return on your extra payment equal to approximately 1/2 of the interest rate you are paying on the loan. This is realized in the next month's reduction of the interest due.
