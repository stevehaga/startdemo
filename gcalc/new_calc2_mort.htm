QuickFacts:

Buying or Refinancing?

Before the 1929 stock market crash, cash purchases of homes were the norm. Or if money was borrowed it was on a term that typically didn't last much more than five years. That made payments relatively steep, which is part of the reason so many homes were lost in the Great Depression. After World War II the U.S. government created a mortgage program that allowed veterans to make affordable payments over a 30-year period. From that need and support has sprung the modern mortgage and all of the different programs

Yet actually mortgages are quite simple to understand as a financial tool for owning real estate.

Lots of choices

Today, the 30-year fixed-rate mortgage, while still the most common way to buy a home, is just one of many financing options available. Indeed, many mortgages today are almost custom-tailored to individual needs. Mortgage lending is a highly competitive field. Information on mortgage rates, which can change daily, is available in local newspapers, the Internet, through mortgage brokers and from individual lenders. When you're shopping for a loan, interest rates tell just part of the story.

The fundamental financial property of a mortgage is the Interest rate. If we know the interest rate and have a loan calculator we can plug in the other known variables and determine the monthly payment. The QuickCalc Mortgage Calculator gives you the opportunity to look at all of these variables and make your own decisions - before you talk to a professional . You can always go to the paper and find out the current rates. But what you need to do is consider all of the variables as they apply to you and your situation.
