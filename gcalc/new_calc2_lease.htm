QuickFacts:

Leasing as Leverage

In today's economic climate, companies and individuals are increasingly challenged to provide funding for essential equipment and services. Leasing can help you fulfill these obligations - and achieve your financial goals. Leasing can provide a flexible and convenient financing alternative with a customized repayment schedule that matches your cash flow.

With a lease you have the option to acquire assets over a fixed term with fixed payments at a fixed, tax-exempt interest rate and have the right to purchase the equipment at the expiration of the term. The annually renewable nature of some leases can allow you to avoid having the financing classified as "debt" for purposes of state law debt limitations. And, by leasing, your yearly expenditures are lower, helping you strengthen your budget and cash flow position.

At the end of the lease contract, the lease gives you the opportunity to buy the equipment on advantageous terms. Usually the lease company retains a security interest until the end of the agreement term.

In the public sector lease financing allows you to take full advantage of your tax-exempt status, which lets you make acquisitions at current prices while enjoying immediate cost savings.

NOTE: Many lease companies have hidden charges which will make payment and interest values very different - be sure to ask for a complete disclosure as to the hidden charges and the methods.

One of the key benefits of a lease is that you can write off all of the payment. With the loan you must depreciate the amortized cost. As in any major investment consult your financial professional to determine which option is the best for you.
